03

Textual Structures

From linear to multilinear — how narrative paths diverge

LINEAR

A fixed sequence of words. Read conventionally from beginning to end.

NONLINEAR

All texts that do not follow Aristotelian laws. The user controls the order.

MULTILINEAR

User-defined pathways branching into different narrative arcs and endings.

The Birth of a Genre

Text Adventures

In 1976, Will Crowther released Colossal Cave Adventure, allowing players to explore caves and interact with puzzles in a text-based environment.

This introduced the concept of a non-linear story where players could choose their path.

05

Chatbots

From Eliza to Galatea — the evolution of conversational AI

ELIZA — 1966

Joseph Weizenbaum’s pioneering chatbot at MIT:

  • Simulated psychotherapeutic conversations
  • Used keyword recognition and pattern matching
  • Responded with predetermined phrases
  • Created a convincing illusion of understanding
GALATEA — 2000

Emily Short’s landmark interactive fiction:

  • Recognizes and adapts to user input
  • Builds unique narratives in real-time
  • Maintains context awareness
  • Remembers past interactions

Key innovation: Personalized collaborative storytelling between AI and humans.

CONCLUSION

Conclusion

“Interactive storytelling merges the ancient art of narrative with the dynamic possibilities of digital media.”

This form invites audiences to step into the story world, making choices that unfold the narrative in unique directions.

At its core, interactive storytelling is about empowering participants to influence the story’s path, making each journey personal.
