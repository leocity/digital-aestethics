03

Textual Structures

From linear to multilinear — how narrative paths diverge

Linear

A fixed sequence of words. Read conventionally from beginning to end.

Nonlinear

All texts that do not follow Aristotelian laws. The user controls the order of stages.

Multilinear

User-defined pathways branching into entirely different narrative arcs and endings.

The Birth of a Genre

Text Adventures

In 1976, Will Crowther released Colossal Cave Adventure, allowing players to explore a network of caves and interact with characters, objects, and puzzles in a text-based environment.

This game introduced the concept of a non-linear story where players could choose their path and make decisions that altered the narrative.

05

Chatbots

From Eliza to Galatea — the evolution of conversational AI

1966

ELIZA

Joseph Weizenbaum’s pioneering chatbot at MIT:

  • Simulated psychotherapeutic conversations
  • Used keyword recognition and pattern matching
  • Responded with predetermined phrases
  • Created a convincing illusion of understanding

ELIZA conversation — Wikimedia Commons

2000

Galatea

Emily Short’s landmark interactive fiction chatbot:

  • Recognizes and adapts to user input
  • Builds unique narratives in real-time
  • Maintains context awareness
  • Remembers past interactions and events

Key innovation: Personalized stories enabling collaborative storytelling between AI and humans.

Jean-Léon Gérôme, Pygmalion and Galatea, c.1890 — Metropolitan Museum of Art

Games · Art · Culture

A MAZE. Berlin

International festival for independent games and playful media, founded in 2008 by Thorsten S. Wiedemann. Held annually in Berlin at silent green Kulturquartier.

  • Celebrates games as art and culture: exhibitions, talks, performances, and the A MAZE. Awards
  • Showcases experimental, boundary-pushing interactive works — from Twine hypertext to VR installations and alt.ctrl games
  • A key bridge between the indie games scene and the digital art world
  • Notable showcases: Baba Is You, Genesis Noir, Mundaun, and many art games and interactive installations

Conclusion

“Interactive storytelling merges the ancient art of narrative with the dynamic possibilities of digital media.”

This emergent form invites audiences to step into the story world, making choices that unfold the narrative in unique directions. It harnesses the interactive capabilities of video games, virtual reality, and online platforms to create immersive experiences where the line between creator and consumer blurs.

At its core, interactive storytelling is about empowering participants to influence the story’s path, making each journey a personal and distinctive experience.
